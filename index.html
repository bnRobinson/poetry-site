Where'd My Pen Go?

Messy Poetry by Brittany Robinson

I didn’t know yet That teen moms weren’t cool, That rap music wasn’t for kids, Or that trailers weren’t homes. City parks are vacations And my bike is freedom. I didn’t know yet Why you ripped up the kitchen floor, Why I was sad to only see dad on Holidays or What it meant to live on a dead end road. But I’ll learn. I didn’t know yet That when someone says “You’re useless” It may not be true. That holes in walls Are not for decoration, Or why you were weary Of the neighbors. I am a big sister, And school is my favorite place. I didn’t know yet What it felt like to be safe all of the time, To be excited for each day that follows, Or to love the simplicity of life. But I’ll learn. I always learn.
